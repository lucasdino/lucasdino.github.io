The short of it

Hi — I'm Lucas, and I'm from Nebraska. The long version of the story involves a finance degree, two years in investment banking, a year lived out of airports and long-term Airbnbs, and a fairly abrupt handbrake turn toward machine learning. The short version is below.

I promise I'm more of a person than a list — but lists do load faster.

2018 – 22
Studied finance at WashU in St. Louis. Four years on the Missouri side of the Mississippi; also where I first picked up a taste for the Midwest-to-elsewhere pipeline.
2022 – 24
Two years in investment banking at FT Partners. Learned a lot, tracked my honest working hours, and eventually realized I wanted to build things rather than model them.
2024
Decided to pivot — if not in your early twenties, when? Wrote up my reasoning here. (No resentment, no grand plan — just a different curiosity.)
2024 – 25
Took a year off to study math, ML, and CS — and to apply to grad school. I studied a lot, and also had a bit of fun — mostly in long-term Airbnbs and pet sits on three continents.
2025 –
NowAt UCSD, doing ML research with the PEARLS Lab. Building a corporate simulation as a multi-turn RL environment for language models. Recently led a paper on how fine-tuning data shapes downstream reasoning in chess — with a talk at NeurIPS 2025.

If any of the above strikes a chord — or you'd just like to say hi — the dispatch desk is open.
