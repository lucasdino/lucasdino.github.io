Research

I care about model behavior, not just model vibes.

I am currently at UC San Diego working on ML and doing research with PEARLS Lab. The throughline in my work is simple: if a model looks capable, I want to know what that capability depends on, how robust it is, and whether it survives longer-horizon interaction.

Featured paper

Reasoning Through Chess

How reasoning evolves from data through fine-tuning and reinforcement learning

I led work showing that the fine-tuning data you choose meaningfully shapes downstream reasoning behavior after RL, even when the task looks as crisp as chess. The project combined custom datasets, careful evaluation, and a focus on qualitative as well as quantitative model behavior.

Current lab direction

A corporate simulation for multi-turn RL

My main project right now is a corporate simulation environment designed for language models that need to reason, plan, use tools, and absorb costs over multiple turns. The goal is to create something with realistic market and competitive dynamics instead of another single-shot proxy task.

Related project

A* neural-guided search on ARC-AGI

I trained an image embedding model from scratch using synthetic data, then combined it with a probabilistic grammar in a neurosymbolic search setup for ARC-AGI. It was a useful test of how much structure you can keep without giving up expressive search.

Philosophize That

Built a corpus of philosophical texts, isolated signal from correlated noise, and trained a model on document-level embeddings to classify the philosophical basis of my own journal entries. Equal parts earnest and unserious.

Research themes

  • Understanding how training data shapes post-RL reasoning behavior.
  • Designing environments that require memory, planning, and meaningful multi-turn tradeoffs.
  • Making evaluation setups harder to game and easier to interpret.
  • Keeping the work explainable enough that people outside the subfield can still follow the argument.
