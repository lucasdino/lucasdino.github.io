The travel was real. The work was too.

I left banking because I wanted to get seriously technical and see if this next chapter actually fit.

Studying from Wisconsin would have worked. Studying from a rotating set of hostels, Airbnbs, pet sits, libraries, and airport floors sounded more memorable.

One of those options clearly had more energy.

The less romantic version is that I also did a lot of focused work. The study log is here.

Best outcome

The pivot held up

I came back more certain, more technical, and with a much better read on what kind of work actually energizes me.

The stories got better

Field note 01

The sabbatical worked because it had constraints

I kept the travel cheap, the routine disciplined, and the goal unambiguous: get technical enough to know whether this new direction deserved the bet.

Field note 02

The work and the fun were not separate lanes

Studying hard by day and then wandering a new city, hiking a mountain, or meeting a few wild people at night made the whole year feel sharper rather than split.

Field note 03

Good life design is still design

I learned a lot about how little I actually need, what environments make me productive, and which kinds of discomfort are worth choosing on purpose.
