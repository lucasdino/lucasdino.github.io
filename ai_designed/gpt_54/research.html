Research

Reasoning, environments, and what data teaches a model to do.

I'm currently working on RL + LLMs with PEARLS Lab. The main thread right now is building a corporate simulation that can act as a multi-turn RL environment for language models.

The goal is to generate realistic tasks in finance, data analysis, and corporate strategy where tool calls have costs and the agent has to think across turns rather than one-shot prompts.

Featured Paper

Reasoning Through Chess

Lead author on work showing how fine-tuning data influences downstream reasoning after reinforcement learning. We outperformed state-of-the-art open-source reasoning models in chess using SFT and RL on a 7B language model.

The part I care about most is not just the benchmark result. It is the relationship between dataset construction, learned transition dynamics, and the style of reasoning that survives RL.
